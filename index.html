Hi, I’m Max, a emerging journalist and digital humanities researcher from Kazakhstan. I’m currently in my fourth year at ITMO University in Saint Petersburg, where I study IT in the Humanities. My work focuses on data-driven storytelling, visualizations, and the intersection of technology and society.

I’m deeply engaged in various research projects within the field of Digital Humanities, from exploring literary texts through quantitative analysis to studying how data can illuminate social and cultural trends. My work combines technology and humanities to better understand complex human experiences. You can explore some of these projects, along with other academic work, here on the site.

I also write for Megabyte — a popular science media where I’ve covered a wide range of topics, from music and technology to social issues. My goal is to make complex ideas accessible to a broader audience, whether through in-depth articles or shorter pieces for social media.

Music is another huge part of my life. I’m passionate about artists like Florence Welch, Hozier, and Twenty One Pilots, and I closely follow events like Eurovision and the Sanremo Festival. Music often serves as an inspiration for my writing, whether I’m exploring its cultural significance or simply sharing my latest favorites.

Feel free to reach out to me on Telegram or Instagram to discuss anything from data analysis to the latest music releases.
